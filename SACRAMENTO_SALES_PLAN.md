## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Start with the problem, not the solution**
   - Understand their pain points first
   - Then show how you solve them

2. **Use local examples**
   - Reference actual Sacramento resources
   - Show local data
   - Use Sacramento scenarios in demos

3. **Make it easy to say yes**
   - Free pilot = low barrier
   - No commitment = no risk
   - Clear ROI = easy to justify

4. **Be patient but persistent**
   - Government moves slowly
   - Follow up regularly (but not annoyingly)
   - Build relationships over time

5. **Get a champion**
   - Find one person who loves it
   - They'll sell it internally for you
   - Makes everything easier

6. **Track everything**
   - Document all conversations
   - Save all emails
   - Record metrics during pilot

## ‚ùì Common Questions & Answers

**Q: How long does the sales process take?**
A: Typically 3-6 months for government contracts. Pilot can start in 2-4 weeks.

**Q: What if they say no?**
A: Ask why. Address concerns. Offer to extend pilot. Consider alternative approach (nonprofit partner, grant application).

**Q: Do I need to be a registered vendor?**
A: Not for the pilot. For full contract, you may need to register. Help them with the process.

**Q: What about data privacy?**
A: You can operate with full privacy. No PII required. Sign data sharing agreement if needed.

**Q: Can I charge more?**
A: Yes, if you can justify it. Focus on ROI, not price. If ROI is strong, price is less important.
